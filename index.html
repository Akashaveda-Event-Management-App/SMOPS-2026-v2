Theme of the Conference

Innovative Operations for Smart and Sustainable Space Mission Management- next Generation.

Connect & Collaborate

This conference is to associate and connect with the various space agencies, start-ups, industry and academia. The conference aims to bring in all space leaders together to share their views on existing technologies and its challenges, while previewing the state-of-the-art technologies that are being conceived globally to make space mission operations more secure and robust.

Global Space Collaboration

Innovation & Technology

Innovation Focus

The scope covers mission operations management, advanced mission design, automation, large constellations management, and present and future trends in ground station operations addressing futuristic techniques. This conference will provide a platform to boost the synergy between start-ups, academia, industry and space agencies; also provide the opportunity for users to interact with service providers.

About Us

Leading organizations driving India's space odyssey

ISRO

Indian Space Research Organisation

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is India's national space agency, established in 1969 under the Department of Space. Headquartered in Bengaluru, ISRO's mission is to harness space technology for national development while pursuing planetary exploration. It has achieved milestones like the Chandrayaan lunar missions, Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission), Aditya-L1 solar mission, and Gaganyaan test missions of human spaceflight program.

Know More

IN-SPACe

Indian National Space Promotion and Authorization Centre

The Indian National Space Promotion and Authorization Centre (IN-SPACe) is an autonomous body under the Department of Space, established in 2020 to promote, regulate, and authorize private sector participation in India's space activities. Serving as a single-window interface between ISRO, industry, startups, and academia, it facilitates access to space infrastructure, technology, and data.

Know More

ASI

Astronautical Society of India

The Astronautical Society of India (ASI), established in 1990, is dedicated to fostering the development of astronautics in India. ASI serves as a bridge between academia, industry, and space agencies, promoting knowledge sharing and collaboration in space sciences. The society organizes various technical events, including SMOPS, bringing together experts from around the world.

Know More

Bengaluru

The Garden City of India

Bengaluru, India's Silicon Valley and the "Garden City," is a perfect blend of technological innovation and natural beauty. Home to ISRO headquarters, HAL, and numerous aerospace startups, it serves as an ideal venue for SMOPS-2026. With its pleasant climate, world-class infrastructure, and vibrant tech ecosystem, Bengaluru offers conference attendees the perfect blend of professional networking opportunities and cultural experiences.
